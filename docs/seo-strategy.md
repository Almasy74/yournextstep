# SEO Strategy

## Keyword Philosophy
Target long-tail "Should I ___?" queries with high decision intent. These queries indicate someone ready to act — not just browsing.

## Category Focus Areas

| Category | Intent Cluster | Search Volume Potential |
|----------|----------------|----------------------|
| career-decisions | Career anxiety, job change, promotion | High |
| ai-and-jobs | AI disruption, automation fear, upskilling | Very High (trending) |
| learning | Course ROI, credential value, skill picks | High |
| money-decisions | Financial choices, investment basics, budgeting | High (YMYL-careful) |
| side-hustles | Income diversification, freelance, passive income | High |

## URL Structure
```
/should-i-[action]-[context]/
```
Examples:
- `/should-i-learn-python-in-2026/`
- `/should-i-quit-my-job-without-another-one/`
- `/should-i-get-a-pmp-certification/`

## Publishing Cadence
- **Weeks 1–4**: 5 pages/day = 140 pages
- **Weeks 5–8**: 3 pages/day = 84 pages (total: ~224)
- **Weeks 9–12**: 2 pages/day + optimization of top performers
- **Target**: 200 pages in first 40 publishing days

## Internal Linking Model

### Hub & Spoke
- **Hubs**: Category index pages (`/career-decisions/`, `/learning/`, etc.)
- **Spokes**: Individual decision pages
- Every spoke links back to its hub via breadcrumbs
- Every spoke links to 2–5 related spokes via `relatedSlugs`

### Cross-Category Links
- Decision pages may reference pages in other categories when relevant
- Example: "Should I learn AI in 2026" (learning) → "Should I switch to a tech career" (career-decisions)

### Pagination
- Category hubs show top 30 pages
- Additional pages at `/career-decisions/page/2/`, `/career-decisions/page/3/`, etc.
- `rel="next/prev"` and correct canonicals on each paginated page

## Structured Data
- **BreadcrumbList**: All pages
- **Article**: All decision pages
- **FAQPage**: Only pages with 5+ substantive FAQ questions
- Conservative usage — no SpeakableSpecification or Review schema

## Technical SEO
- Canonical URLs with trailing slash
- `<meta robots>` noindex on draft/unpublished pages
- `sitemap.xml` auto-generated by build
- `robots.txt` allows all crawlers
- Static HTML (no JS rendering required for indexing)
- `<lastmod>` dates from `updatedDate` field

## Content Quality Signals
- Reading time displayed on every page
- Updated date visible
- Sources cited
- Methodology page linked (/how-scoring-works.html)
- Disclaimer and editorial transparency
